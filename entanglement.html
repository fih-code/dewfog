quantum entanglement

superposition · correlation · nonlocality
Two particles can be created as a linked pair, with a property called spin that isn't set until someone measures it. The moment you measure one particle, you instantly know the other's result too — even if it's on the other side of the galaxy. This strange link has been tested and confirmed again and again in real labs, and it rules out any simple, ordinary explanation.
