looking for the looker

attention · reversed · unfound
Every meditation technique points attention outward, or inward at some content — a breath, a sound, a thought. Self-inquiry does something stranger: it turns attention around to look for the one doing the looking. Across traditions with no contact with each other — Ramana Maharshi's "who am I," Zen's "who is hearing this sound," Douglas Harding's headless way — practitioners report the same specific finding: no separate observer is ever found, only more looking. One reading says that's because awareness isn't an object at all — it's the backdrop everything else appears in. Philosopher Thomas Metzinger's reading says it's because a well-built self-model is supposed to be looked through, not found. Trying this yourself won't tell you which is right — both explanations predict exactly what you'll experience.
