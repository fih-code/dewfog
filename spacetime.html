spacetime curvature

mass · geodesic · dilation
Einstein's theory of general relativity says gravity isn't really a force at all — it's the bending of space and time caused by mass. Objects don't get pulled toward each other; they simply follow the straightest possible path through space that's been curved by something heavy nearby. One strange result: clocks run slightly slower the closer they are to a massive object. GPS satellites have to correct for this tiny effect, or their location data would drift off within minutes.
