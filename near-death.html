near death

void · light · return
Some people who survive cardiac arrest report a near-death experience — a tunnel, a bright light, a sense of returning from somewhere else. It's not the typical outcome; most survivors report nothing like it. Scientists have proposed a few possible causes: the brain reacting to low oxygen, dream-like activity intruding into waking consciousness, or a flood of the brain's own chemicals. Whatever the cause, the experience shows up in strikingly similar form across very different cultures, even in people who had no prior belief in it.
