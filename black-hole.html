black hole

horizon · radiation · paradox
As matter spirals into a black hole, it heats up to millions of degrees. Past a boundary called the event horizon, gravity is so strong that not even light can escape. In 1974, physicist Stephen Hawking showed that black holes leak a faint stream of radiation and slowly shrink over time. What actually happens to anything that falls in — destroyed forever, or somehow preserved — is still an open question.
